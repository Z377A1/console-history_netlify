The PlayStation 5 is a home video game console developed by Sony Interactive Entertainment. ([wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_5))

Details:
- Name for Console: PlayStation 5
- Alias: PS5
- Year Released: 2020
- Discontinued: Ongoing
- Developer: Sony Interactive Entertainment
- Product Family: PlayStation
- Generation Number: 9th Generation
- Backward Compatibility: Yes
    - [PlayStation 4](../playstation-4)
- Specifications:
    - Operating System: Customized FreeBSD
    - CPU: Custom AMD Zen 2
    - GPU: Custom AMD RDNA 2
    - Memory: 16 GB GDDR6
    - Storage: 825 GB NVMe SSD
    - Display Formats: 1080p, 1440p, 4K, 8K
    - Sound: Tempest 3D AudioTech
    - Controller Input: DualSense Wireless Controller
    - Extra: Comes in Disc or Digital, same Specifications. In November, 2023 Sony released the [PlayStation 5 Slim](https://blog.playstation.com/2023/10/10/new-look-for-ps5-console-this-holiday-season/) with a swappable disc drive and a 1 TB SSD.
- Units Sold: 40+ Million Units (As of [2023](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/playstation-5-sales-surpass-40m-worldwide))
- Predecessor: [PlayStation 4](../playstation-4)
- Slim Refresh: [PlayStation 5 Slim](https://blog.playstation.com/2023/10/10/new-look-for-ps5-console-this-holiday-season/)
- Gen Refresh: None
- Successor: None
